Module 3: ITSM Frameworks and Standards

Unit Focus:

Explore widely used ITSM frameworks such as ITIL, COBIT, and ISO/IEC 20000. Understand the benefits of adopting standardised frameworks in enhancing IT governance, service quality, and operational efficiency.

Required Reading:

  • Weed-Schertzer, B. (2019). Delivering ITSM for Business Maturity: A Practical Framework. Chapter 4.
  • Isaychenko, D. & Demin, P. (2022). Metrics-based IT Service Management. London: The Stationery Office Ltd.
  • Salehi, A. & Vazife, Z. (2019). The Effect of the Implementation of ISMS and ITIL on Information Systems Continuity. pp. 225–249.
  • Kumbakara, N. (2008). Managed IT Services: The Role of IT Standards. Information Management & Computer Security, 336–359.

Additional Reading:

  • Bon, J. van & Verheijen, T. (2006). Foundations of IT Service Management: Based on ITIL. Van Haren Publishing.
  • Office of Government Commerce (OGC). (2011). ITIL Service Lifecycle Suite.

Activity:

Compare and contrast at least two ITSM frameworks. Discuss how aligning IT operations with a formal framework can support compliance, audit readiness, and continual service improvement.
